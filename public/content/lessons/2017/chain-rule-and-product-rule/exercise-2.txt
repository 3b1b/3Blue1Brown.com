$(x^3 + 2x)^2$

$$
f(x) = (x^3 + 2x)^2
$$

$$
f(x) = g(h(x))
$$

Start by setting up the derivative of the function.

$$
\frac{d}{dx} \left( f(x) \right) = \frac{d}{dx}\left((x^3 + 2x)^2\right)
$$

Recognize that this function fits the chain rule pattern. The inner function's output, let's call it $h = x^3 + 2x$, gets fed into the outer functions input.

$$
h = x^3 + 2x
$$

$$
g = h^2
$$

$$
f(x) = \frac{d}{dh}\left(h^2\right) \cdot \frac {dh}{dx}
$$
